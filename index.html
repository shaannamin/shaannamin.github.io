About

Shaan Amin is an Indian American essayist, journalist, critic, and researcher from Syracuse and Elmira, New York. He received his MA and PhD in political science from the University of Wisconsin-Madison, where he received a National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellowship for outstanding research and a Department Teaching Excellence Award for significant contributions in instruction. He served on the editorial board at The Rumpus from 2021-2025. Before this, he received his BA in Mathematics from Williams College.

He is currently working on two book projects. The State of Prejudice, based on his dissertation of the same name, records and analyzes how local governments and local NGOs provide and fail to provide services to Indonesia's most at-risk populations. Homespun, an in-progress creative nonfiction essay collection, unpacks the lived, historical, and mythical experience of injuries and violence blending the tools of historical research, personal anecdote, and myth.

In his spare time, he works at Bhakti Yoga DC, hosts a weekly open potluck, and is a dedicated member of the vibrant DC salsa and bachata scene.
